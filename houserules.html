House Rules

General

  • Must verify sober/clean before arrival.
  • Must submit to initial and random screenings.
    • - Tests are through urinary analysis.
    - Resident is responsible for the cost of their test(s), $5.00.
    - If not paid at time of test, price is $10.00.
  • Rent is due on or before the 1st of each month.
    • - $10 of each on-time payment will be donated to a house budget for common use items.
    - Late payments are charged $10 per day.
    - Resident is subject to automatic eviction if not paid by the 5th fo the month.
  • For the safety of the residents, this is a male only facility.
    • - Weekdays: No non-residents are permitted after 11:00 PM or your designated curfew.
    - Weekends: No non-residents are permitted after 12:00 PM (midnight) or your designated curfew.
    - Non-residents are only permitted in common areas or your room (door must remain open).
    - Non-residents must have the permission of the House President prior to entering the House.
    - No excessive sexual or physical contact is permitted.
    - Residents must remain fully clothed in common areas (no bare feet, socks are ok).
  • Entry and exit doors to the House must be locked at all times.
  • Residents will be given individual lock codes or keys and may not share the code or keys with any resident or non-resident.
    • - Lost keys are $15.00 for eah replacement.
    - Sharing access codes or keys will result in one (1) violation.
  • Smoking is allowed in designated areas only.
    • - Smoking is NEVER permitted in the house.
  • All residents are entitled to their own private space.
    • Another resident may NOT enter another residents private space without permission.
  • There is a zero tolerance policy for the following. Any violation will result in immedate eviction.
    • - Theft of any kind.
    - Violence.
    - Harassment of any kind and to include, race, religion, sexual orientation.
    - Threatening another resident.
    - Possesion of any weapon.
    (Personal knives must be approved by the House President.)
  • No bedroom doors will be locked.
  • No loud music that would disturb others or the neighbors.
  • Residents are responsble to bring their own personal lock box if needed.
  • Residents are not permitted to make any material changes to the House.
    • - Hanging personal pictures is permitted within your personal space only.
  • Sanus House and it's employees and representatives are not responsible for any theft, damage or harm to any of the residents.
  • Sanus House and it's employees and representatives may enter the property at any time for inspection, maintenance or any other time as necessary.
  • For medical or other emergencies of serious harm or danger call 911.
  • For emergency repairs contact the House President immedately, if unavailable, you can call Sanus House management at (385) 350-3013 24/7.
  • For other repairs and maintenance contact the House Presient.

First 30 Days

  • All residents are required to enrole in a 12 step program and obtain sponsorship.
    • - One (1) violation will be issued for each week the resident remains non compliant.
  • All residents must attend five (5) weekly 12 step program meetings.
    • - All attendance must be recorded and signed.
  • All residents must checkin daily with the designated House President
    • - This can be by phone or in person
    - It must be an auditable contact. Text messages are not sufficient.
  • Curfew
    • - Weekdays: 8:00 PM.
    - Weekends: 10:00 PM.

Post 30 Days

  • All residents must attend three (3) weekly 12 step program meetings.
    • - All attendance must be recorded and signed.
  • Curfew
    • - Weekdays: 10:00 PM.
    - Weekends: 120:00 PM (midnight).

Extended Absenses

  • Only permitted after 30 days and with approval from the House (unanimous vote of all residents).
    • - Emergency requests will be handled on a case by case basis by the House President.
  • Must be logged, duration and location.
  • UA is required upon return.

Medication

  • Must be reported to the House President and recorded.
  • Must be kept in a locked container. (Resident's responsiblity.)

Hygiene

  • Shower regularly
  • Brush Teeth
  • Wear deodorant
  • Keep you clothing clean

Pets

  • No pets are allowed.

Chores

  • Will be assigned by the House.
  • Are to be logged and reported as complete.
  • Are to be rotated.

Keeping Order in the House

Common Area

  • No sleeping in the common area.
  • No furniture may be moved to other rooms or bedrooms.
  • Furniture may be rearranged as needed or liked with the consent of the other residents.
  • Make room for others.
  • Last person to leave the room turns off the lights.
  • Thermostat is controlled by the House President.
  • The TV must be kept at a moderate level.
  • The computer is for everyone's use and may not be locked with a password.
  • Any disagreements, the House President has final say.

Kitchen

  • All food must be kept in sealed containers.
    • - No raw fish other than canned tuna.
    - Fruits and vegetables may sit on the counter if they are kept contained.
  • No food or other items are to be left out on the counters.
  • Refrigerator must be kept clean of old and spoiled food.
  • Dishes must be rinsed and stacked next to the sink to be washed.
    • - No dishes are to be left in the sink.

Bedrooms

  • Bedding must be kept clean and used.
  • Clothing must be hung up, in your dresser or in a laundry bin.
  • You are only permitted the use of your designated space.
  • Floors are to remain uncluttered and clean.
  • You may hang pictures and other items within your personal space only.
  • Electric or alternate heaters are NOT permitted.
    • - Electric blankets are okay.
  • Small fans are okay.

Bathrooms

  • No toiletries to to be left out on the counter or in the shower/tub.
  • Counters, toilets, tubs/showers floors are to be kept clean.
  • Clean up after yourself.

Laundry

  • Is provided to all residents.
  • Residents are expected to work out a schedule that works for the House.
  • As funds permit the House budget will provide detergent, etc.
  • Must be removed promptly after the cycle ends.
    • - Residents are NOT to remove the clothing of other residents.

Garage

  • Is common area.
  • Parking is to be worked out by the House.
  • Personal items such as tools should be in a locked box.
  • No smoking is permitted.

Yard (fenced area)

  • Is common area.
  • Will have designated smoking areas.
  • Must be kept clean and orderly.

Parking (and outside the fenced area)

  • Is common area.
  • Is a designated smoking area, but must be alway from the cars.
  • No inoperable vehicles are permitted unless being actively worked on.
  • No residents are permitted beyond the South fence.
  • The trailers, sheds and other buildings are not to be entered or disturbed.
    • - Doing so will result in a violation and could result in immedate eviction.
  • No fires are permitted.
  • Fireworks are permitted only with the approval of the House and according to permitted use.

Accountability

Notices

Are associated with minor infractions. Three (3) notices make one (1) violation.
  • Failue to complete a chore.
  • Clothing left in the laundry area longer than 1 hour.
  • Demonstrating disrespect, serious verbal altercations, general insubordination

Violations

  • Failure to comply
    • - Instructions given by House President.
    - Stipulations and rules set by the House.
  • Unexcused absense from manditory House meetings
